1. Earthquake Scales:
    - Richter Scale: Measures the magnitude of earthquakes.
      - Example values:
        - `0.0 - 2.0`: Micro (Not felt)
        - `2.0 - 4.0`: Minor (Generally not felt but recorded)
        - `4.0 - 6.0`: Light to Moderate (Noticeable shaking, but minor damage)
        - `6.0 - 8.0`: Strong to Major (Potential for severe damage)
        - `8.0+`: Great (Total destruction in affected areas)

    - Moment Magnitude Scale: Similar to the Richter Scale but used for measuring larger quakes.
      - Example values:
        - `0.0 - 2.0`: Micro
        - `2.0 - 4.0`: Small
        - `4.0 - 6.0`: Medium
        - `6.0 - 8.0`: Large
        - `8.0+`: Huge

    - Mercalli Scale: Measures the intensity of shaking on a scale from I to XII.
      - Example values:
        - `I`: Not felt
        - `III`: Weak shaking
        - `V`: Moderate shaking
        - `VII`: Strong shaking
        - `IX`: Violent shaking
        - `XII`: Total destruction

2. Flood Scales:
    - Flood Stage: Indicates the severity of flooding.
      - Example values:
        - `0 - 10`: Normal flow
        - `10 - 20`: Action Stage
        - `20 - 30`: Minor Flooding
        - `30 - 40`: Moderate Flooding
        - `40+`: Major Flooding

    - Inundation Level: Measures the depth of water in meters or feet.
      - Example values:
        - `0 - 1 meter (3.3 feet)`: Low risk
        - `1 - 3 meters (3.3 - 10 feet)`: Medium risk
        - `3+ meters (10+ feet)`: High risk

3. Hurricane Scales:
    - Saffir-Simpson Scale: Measures hurricane strength by wind speed.
      - Example values:
        - `Category 1`: 74-95 mph (Minor damage)
        - `Category 2`: 96-110 mph (Moderate damage)
        - `Category 3`: 111-129 mph (Devastating damage)
        - `Category 4`: 130-156 mph (Catastrophic damage)
        - `Category 5`: 157+ mph (Complete destruction)

    - Beaufort Scale: Measures wind speed from 0 to 12.
      - Example values:
        - `0`: Calm (< 1 mph)
        - `3`: Gentle breeze (7-10 mph)
        - `6`: Strong breeze (25-31 mph)
        - `9`: Strong gale (47-54 mph)
        - `12`: Hurricane force (73+ mph)

4. Tornado Scales:
    - Enhanced Fujita Scale: Rates tornado intensity based on damage caused.
      - Example values:
        - `EF0`: 65-85 mph (Minor damage)
        - `EF1`: 86-110 mph (Moderate damage)
        - `EF2`: 111-135 mph (Considerable damage)
        - `EF3`: 136-165 mph (Severe damage)
        - `EF4`: 166-200 mph (Devastating damage)
        - `EF5`: 200+ mph (Incredible damage)

    - TORRO Scale: Measures tornado strength, ranging from T0 to T11.
      - Example values:
        - `T0 - T1`: Weak tornado (Minor damage)
        - `T2 - T3`: Moderate tornado (Moderate damage)
        - `T4 - T5`: Strong tornado (Severe damage)
        - `T6 - T7`: Very strong tornado (Devastating damage)
        - `T8 - T10`: Violent tornado (Massive damage)
        - `T11`: Super tornado (Unprecedented damage)

5. Wildfire Scales:
    - Fire Danger Index (FDI): Measures potential fire danger.
      - Example values:
        - `0 - 5`: Low risk
        - `6 - 12`: Moderate risk
        - `13 - 25`: High risk
        - `26 - 50`: Very high risk
        - `50+`: Extreme risk

    - Burn Area Index (BAI): Estimates the burn severity based on satellite data.
      - Example values:
        - `0 - 50`: Low severity
        - `51 - 100`: Medium severity
        - `101+`: High severity
